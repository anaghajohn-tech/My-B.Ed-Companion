Q.1 a) What is communication? b) What are the types of communication? c) Explain the factors of non-verbal communication that influence the teaching-learning process. (2+3+5) ▾
a) What is Communication? (2 marks)
Communication is the process of transmitting information, ideas, thoughts, feelings and emotions from one person to another through a medium. It involves a sender, message, channel, receiver and feedback. In education, communication is the exchange of knowledge and understanding between teacher and students.
b) Types of Communication (3 marks)
  • Verbal Communication: Using spoken or written words — lectures, discussions, textbooks, blackboard writing
  • Non-verbal Communication: Using body language, gestures, facial expressions, posture, eye contact, appearance, space (proxemics)
  • Visual Communication: Using charts, diagrams, graphs, films, displays
  • Electronic/Digital Communication: Using email, internet, multimedia, video conferencing
  • One-way vs Two-way: Broadcasting (one-way) vs interactive communication (two-way with feedback)
c) Factors of Non-Verbal Communication Influencing Teaching-Learning (5 marks)
  • Facial Expressions: A teacher's smile, frown, or raised eyebrow sends powerful signals about approval, disapproval or expectations — students respond accordingly
  • Eye Contact: Maintaining eye contact with students shows interest and keeps them engaged; avoiding it may signal lack of confidence or disinterest
  • Gestures: Hand movements, pointing, nodding — emphasise key points and make explanations more vivid and memorable
  • Posture and Body Language: Open posture (facing class, leaning forward) signals enthusiasm; closed posture (arms folded, turning away) creates distance
  • Proxemics (Space): Moving closer to students signals attention and concern; standing far away at all times creates emotional distance
  • Para-language (Voice): Tone, pitch, volume, pace — a monotone voice loses students; varied intonation maintains interest and emphasises important ideas
  • Appearance: Teacher's dress and grooming send messages about professionalism and create first impressions that influence student respect
  • Silence: Purposeful silence after a question gives students thinking time and shows the teacher values thoughtful responses
Q.2 a) What is computer assisted instruction? b) Explain briefly the types of computer assisted instruction. c) Mention the role of the teacher in computer assisted instructions. (2+4+4) ▾
a) What is Computer Assisted Instruction? (2 marks)
Computer Assisted Instruction (CAI) is an interactive instructional technique where a computer is used to present instructional material, monitor student learning, and provide immediate feedback. It allows students to learn at their own pace through programmed content delivered via the computer.
★ From 2016 Official Scheme (Prof. B.R. Gopal): CAI "is for teaching" — distinguishing it from Computer Adaptive Testing which "is for testing".
b) Types of CAI (4 marks)
  • Drill and Practice: Presents a series of problems/questions for the student to practice. Provides immediate feedback — correct/incorrect. Used for reinforcing already-taught skills (e.g., multiplication tables, spelling). Best for mastery of routine skills.
  • Tutorial: Presents new information to the student step by step, asks questions, and branches based on responses. Acts as a substitute teacher. Includes explanation, examples, and assessment in one sequence.
  • Simulation: Creates a model of a real-world system or process. Students interact with the simulation to explore concepts safely (e.g., virtual chemistry lab, flight simulator). Develops problem-solving and decision-making skills.
  • Games: Educational games that teach concepts through competitive, engaging play. Motivates students through challenge and reward. Teaches strategic thinking alongside subject content.
  • Problem-solving: Presents complex problems requiring the student to apply reasoning and knowledge. Develops higher-order thinking skills.
c) Role of Teacher in CAI (4 marks)
  • Planner/Designer: Selects appropriate CAI programs aligned with curriculum objectives; designs learning sequences
  • Facilitator: Guides students through the CAI process; answers questions the computer cannot address
  • Monitor: Observes student progress through the CAI system; identifies students who need additional help
  • Motivator: Encourages students who feel frustrated or bored; maintains enthusiasm for computer-based learning
  • Evaluator: Assesses effectiveness of CAI programs used; uses data generated by the computer to inform teaching
  • Technical Manager: Ensures hardware and software are functioning; troubleshoots basic technical problems
Q.3 a) What is computer? b) Explain the role of computer in education. c) Briefly explain the types of computer software. (1+4+5) ▾
a) What is a Computer? (1 mark)
A computer is an electronic machine or device that accepts data (input), processes it according to stored instructions (a program), and produces results (output). It can store large amounts of information and perform calculations rapidly and accurately.
b) Role of Computer in Education (4 marks)
  • Enables individualised/self-paced learning through CAI programs tailored to each student's pace and level
  • Provides immediate feedback — students know right away if their answers are correct
  • Acts as a teaching tool — multimedia presentations, simulations, virtual labs
  • Facilitates assessment — Computer Adaptive Testing adjusts difficulty based on student responses
  • Supports teacher preparation — lesson plans, question papers, result analysis
  • Enables access to global resources via internet — enriching educational content
  • Develops students' technological literacy — essential for the 21st century
c) Types of Computer Software (5 marks)
  • System Software: Controls and manages the hardware and other software. Includes Operating Systems (Windows, macOS, Linux, Android) and utility programs.
  • Application Software: Programs designed for specific user tasks. Includes word processors (MS Word), spreadsheets (Excel), presentations (PowerPoint), databases (Access).
  • Programming Software: Tools used by developers to write programs — compilers, interpreters, text editors, IDEs (e.g., Python IDLE, VS Code).
  • Educational Software: Specifically designed for learning — CAI programs, simulations, encyclopaedias, e-learning platforms.
  • Freeware/Open-source Software: Available free of cost — GeoGebra, LibreOffice, Mozilla Firefox. Important for schools with limited budgets.

Q.7 a) What is computer adaptive testing? b) List out the advantages of computer adaptive testing. (2+3) ▾
a) What is Computer Adaptive Testing (CAT)?
Computer Adaptive Testing (CAT) is a computer-based assessment method that adjusts the difficulty of questions based on the test-taker's responses. If the student answers correctly, the next question is harder; if incorrectly, the next is easier. This provides a more precise and efficient measurement of ability compared to fixed tests.
★ From 2016 Scheme: CAT "is for testing" — distinguishing it from CAI which "is for teaching".
b) Advantages of CAT:
  • Efficient: Requires fewer questions than fixed tests to achieve the same measurement accuracy
  • Precise: Provides a more accurate estimate of student ability by targeting questions at the right level
  • Immediate results: Scores and reports available instantly after completion
  • Reduced test anxiety: Students are not overwhelmed by questions far above or below their level
  • Fair: Each student gets questions appropriate to their level — not too easy or too hard
  • Secure: Large item banks mean different students get different questions — reduces cheating
Q.8 Explain the basic principles of programmed instruction. (5) ▾
★ From 2016 Official Scheme: "The five basic principles are: Principle of small steps, principle of active responding, principle of immediate confirmation, principle of self pacing and principle of student testing."
  • Principle of Small Steps: Learning material is broken into very small, manageable units (frames). Each frame presents one idea only, ensuring gradual progress without overwhelming the learner.
  • Principle of Active Responding: The learner must actively respond to each frame — fill in a blank, choose an answer, or solve a problem. Passive reading is not sufficient; engagement is required at every step.
  • Principle of Immediate Confirmation (Feedback): After each response, the correct answer is immediately revealed. This reinforces correct responses and corrects errors before they are reinforced.
  • Principle of Self-Pacing: Each learner proceeds at their own pace — faster learners are not held back, slower learners are not rushed. The system accommodates individual differences.
  • Principle of Student Testing (Evaluation): The program regularly tests student progress. The test results are used to evaluate not just the student but also the effectiveness of the program itself.

Q.1 a) Define Communication. b) What are the barriers of communication in the classroom? c) As a teacher how will you overcome those barriers? (2+4+4) ▾
a) Definition of Communication (2 marks)
Communication is the process of sharing information, ideas, feelings and thoughts between a sender and receiver through a medium, with the aim of creating mutual understanding. In education, effective communication between teacher and student is essential for learning to occur.
b) Barriers of Communication in the Classroom (4 marks)
  • Physical Barriers: Noise, poor acoustics, large class size, poor seating arrangement, inadequate lighting
  • Language Barriers: Use of difficult vocabulary, regional language differences, technical jargon not understood by students
  • Psychological Barriers: Student fear, anxiety, low self-esteem, prejudice or pre-conceived notions about the teacher or subject
  • Cultural Barriers: Different cultural backgrounds affecting interpretation of gestures, examples, or references used
  • Perceptual Barriers: Students and teacher perceiving the same information differently based on background, experience
  • Attitudinal Barriers: Lack of interest, poor motivation, negative attitude towards the subject or teacher
  • Timing Barriers: Lesson delivered when students are tired, hungry or distracted
c) How a Teacher Can Overcome Barriers (4 marks)
  • Use simple, clear language appropriate to students' level; define unfamiliar terms
  • Create a supportive, non-threatening classroom environment where students feel safe to ask questions
  • Use multiple modes of communication — verbal explanation plus visual aids, demonstrations
  • Arrange seating to maximise visibility and reduce physical barriers
  • Use eye contact and movement around the room to maintain attention
  • Encourage feedback and questions — check understanding regularly
  • Be aware of cultural sensitivity in examples, gestures and references used

Q.3 a) What is programmed instruction? b) Briefly explain the types of programmed instruction. c) List the characteristics of programmed instruction. (2+6+2) ▾
a) What is Programmed Instruction? (2 marks)
Programmed Instruction (PI) is a planned sequence of learning experiences leading to proficiency in a skill or knowledge area, based on stimulus-response relationships that have been proven to be effective. It presents content in small frames, requires active student responding, and provides immediate feedback. Based on Skinner's operant conditioning theory.
★ From 2016 Official Scheme: "a planned sequence of experiences, leading to proficiency, in terms of stimulus-response relationship"
b) Types of Programmed Instruction (6 marks)
1. Linear Programmed Instruction (Skinner):
  • All students follow the same sequence of frames regardless of responses
  • Small steps with constructed responses (fill in the blank)
  • Positive reinforcement through immediate feedback
  • Designed so students make very few errors (errorless learning)
2. Branched Programmed Instruction (Crowder):
  • Students follow different paths based on their responses
  • Uses multiple-choice questions; each answer leads to a different frame
  • Correct answer → move forward; Wrong answer → remedial branch with explanation
  • Allows for individual differences — faster learners skip remedial material
  • More flexible and personalised than linear PI
c) Characteristics of Programmed Instruction (2 marks)
  • Small steps / frames of content
  • Active responding required from learner
  • Immediate feedback and reinforcement
  • Self-pacing — learner controls rate of progress
  • Logical, sequential arrangement of content
  • Tested and validated before use

Q.7 Write the meaning and significance of video-lessons. (1+4) ▾
What are Video Lessons?
Video lessons are pre-recorded or live teaching sessions delivered through video technology (DVD, YouTube, NPTEL, SWAYAM, etc.) that combine visual and audio to present educational content.
Significance of Video Lessons:
  • Provides visual and audio stimulation simultaneously — enhances retention significantly
  • Students can pause, rewind and rewatch — supports self-paced and revision learning
  • Expert teachers can reach millions of students simultaneously — democratises quality education
  • Makes learning available anytime, anywhere — flexible learning beyond classroom hours
  • Can demonstrate experiments, procedures, historical events that cannot be reproduced in class
  • Useful for absent students to catch up on missed content
  • Combined with smart class technology, creates immersive learning experiences
Q.8 What is operating system? Briefly explain its types. (2+3) ▾
What is an Operating System?
An Operating System (OS) is system software that manages the computer's hardware resources and provides common services for application programs. It acts as an interface between the user and the computer hardware.
Types of Operating Systems:
  • Single-user, Single-tasking: Allows only one user to do one task at a time (e.g., early MS-DOS)
  • Single-user, Multi-tasking: One user can run multiple programs simultaneously (e.g., Windows, macOS)
  • Multi-user: Multiple users can access the computer simultaneously (e.g., Unix, Linux servers)
  • Real-time OS: Processes data as it arrives with minimal delay — used in medical equipment, control systems
  • Mobile OS: Designed for smartphones and tablets (e.g., Android, iOS)

Educational Technology is the systematic application of scientific knowledge about learning and the conditions of learning to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of teaching and training. (AECT definition) It encompasses hardware, software, and a systems approach to education.
b) Four Objectives of Educational Technology (2 marks)
  • To construct a definite specific pattern to help teaching and learning
  • To shape new learning situations and instructional programs
  • To provide learning opportunity to deprived communities at lower cost
  • To self-guide and organise instructional materials
  • To provide equal opportunity to all classrooms
  • To provide higher technology to even ordinary children
★ From 2016 Official Scheme, Prof. B.R. Gopal
c) Components of Educational Technology (6 marks)
  • Hardware Component: Physical equipment used in education — computers, projectors, smart boards, tablets, audio-visual equipment, laboratory instruments. Hardware provides the medium through which educational content is delivered.
  • Software Component: Programs and digital content — educational software (CAI programs, simulations), multimedia content, e-books, online courses, learning management systems (LMS). Software provides the actual instructional content and tools.
  • Systems Approach (Process Component): A planned, organised methodology for designing, implementing and evaluating instruction. Includes instructional design models (ADDIE), needs analysis, objective setting, content organisation, evaluation. This is the "methodology" dimension of educational technology.
  • Human Component: Teachers, students, instructional designers, technicians who use, manage and benefit from educational technology. The most important component — technology is only as effective as the people who use it.
  • Communication Networks: Internet, intranet, LAN, cloud platforms that connect learners and resources — enabling e-learning, distance education, collaborative learning.

Q.4 Explain briefly the components of non-verbal communication. (5) ▾
  • Facial Expressions: Expressions of happiness, surprise, anger, sadness, fear — these are largely universal and communicate emotions more powerfully than words

  • Para-language (Vocal cues): Voice qualities — tone, pitch, pace, volume, pauses — these modify the meaning of spoken words enormously
  • Appearance and Artifacts: Clothing, grooming, accessories send messages about identity, status and professionalism
  • Touch (Haptics): Handshakes, pats on the back communicate warmth, authority, comfort — though culturally variable

  • Sender (Source): The person who initiates communication; encodes the message from their thoughts
  • Encoding: Converting thoughts/ideas into symbols (words, images, gestures) that can be transmitted
  • Message: The content being communicated — information, ideas, feelings
  • Channel (Medium): The means of transmission — speech, writing, electronic media, body language
  • Receiver: The person who receives the message; decodes it using their background knowledge
  • Decoding: Interpreting the received symbols back into meaning — affected by receiver's knowledge, culture, state of mind
  • Feedback: Receiver's response to the sender — confirms whether the message was understood correctly
  • Noise: Any interference at any stage that distorts the message — physical, psychological, semantic

Q.8 Explain briefly the types of Computer Hardware. (5) ▾
  • Input Devices: Allow user to enter data into the computer — keyboard, mouse, microphone, scanner, webcam, touchscreen, joystick
  • Output Devices: Display or produce results from the computer — monitor, printer, speakers, projector, headphones
  • Processing Unit (CPU): The "brain" of the computer — Central Processing Unit performs all calculations and logical operations. Includes ALU and Control Unit.
  • Primary Memory (RAM/ROM): RAM (Random Access Memory) — temporary, volatile storage for currently running programs. ROM (Read Only Memory) — permanent storage for boot instructions.
  • Secondary Storage: Permanent, long-term storage — hard disk drive (HDD), solid state drive (SSD), USB flash drives, CD/DVD drives, memory cards
  • Motherboard: The main circuit board connecting all components; contains slots for CPU, RAM, and expansion cards

Q.1 a) What is Programmed Instruction? b) Explain briefly the basic principles of Programmed Instruction. c) Mention the differences between Linear and Branching Programmed Instruction. (2+5+3) ▾
a) Meaning of Programmed Instruction — OFFICIAL SCHEME
★ OFFICIAL SCHEME: "Programmed instruction is a planned sequence of experiences, leading to proficiency, in terms of stimulus-response relationship with have proven to be effective."
Programmed Instruction is a planned sequence of experiences, leading to proficiency, in terms of stimulus-response relationships that have proven to be effective. Based on Skinner's operant conditioning, it breaks learning into small frames, requires active responding, and provides immediate reinforcement.
b) Five Basic Principles — OFFICIAL SCHEME
★ OFFICIAL SCHEME: "The five basic principles are: Principle of small steps, principle of active responding, principle of immediate confirmation, principle of self pacing and principle of student testing. If they mention five points give half mark, students explain all the five give full allotted marks."
  • Principle of Small Steps: Content divided into very small, manageable frames — one concept per frame — ensuring gradual, errorless progress
  • Principle of Active Responding: Student must respond at each frame — fill in blank, answer question. Active participation at every step.
  • Principle of Immediate Confirmation (Feedback): Correct answer shown immediately after student responds — reinforces correct learning, corrects errors instantly
  • Principle of Self-Pacing: Each student works at their own pace — no external pressure; accommodates individual differences
  • Principle of Student Testing (Evaluation): Regular testing built into the program — evaluates both student achievement and the program's effectiveness
c) Differences — OFFICIAL SCHEME
★ OFFICIAL SCHEME: "Mention the difference between liner and branching programmed instruction about three points each on their experiences and nature of instruction."
Linear (Skinner)Branched (Crowder)
Same path for all studentsDifferent paths based on responses
Constructed response (fill in blank)Multiple choice responses
Very small steps, near-errorlessLarger steps; errors guide to remedial frames
Q.2 a) Define Educational Technology. b) Explain the hardware, software and systems approach components of Educational Technology. (2+8) ▾
a) Meaning of Educational Technology — OFFICIAL SCHEME
★ OFFICIAL SCHEME: "Meaning of Educational Technology in Brief either with definition or in their own words about three or four sentences."
Educational Technology is the systematic application of scientific knowledge about teaching and learning to improve the effectiveness and efficiency of the educational process. It involves three major components — hardware, software, and a systems approach — working together to achieve learning objectives.
b) Three Components — OFFICIAL SCHEME
★ OFFICIAL SCHEME: "Meaning of hardware, software and system approach components students has to write with example for all the three in brief."
Hardware Component:
  • Physical equipment used in teaching — projectors, smart boards, computers, tablets, audio systems, lab equipment
  • Enables delivery of educational content through machines
  • Examples: Computer lab, smart classroom projector, language lab
Software Component:
  • Programs, digital content and instructional materials used in education
  • Includes CAI programs, educational simulations, e-books, multimedia content, LMS platforms
  • Examples: Khan Academy, SWAYAM, GeoGebra, PowerPoint presentations
Systems Approach:
  • A planned, systematic methodology for designing, implementing and evaluating instruction
  • Treats teaching as a system with interrelated components — objectives, content, methods, media, evaluation
  • Uses models like ADDIE (Analysis, Design, Development, Implementation, Evaluation)
  • Ensures instruction is organised, purposeful and continuously improved
Q.3 a) What is Computer Assisted Instruction and Computer Adaptive Testing? b) Explain in brief the different modes of Computer Assisted Instruction. (4+6) ▾
a) CAI and CAT — OFFICIAL SCHEME
★ OFFICIAL SCHEME: "Meaning of Computer Assisted Instruction and Computer Adoptive Testing in brief, first is for teaching and second one is for testing because now a day most of the work is based on computer both teaching and testing of students achievement so both are essential in the process of learning."
CAI (Computer Assisted Instruction) is the use of a computer to present and manage learning — it is for teaching. CAT (Computer Adaptive Testing) adjusts question difficulty based on student performance — it is for testing. Both are essential because modern education increasingly relies on computers for both instructional delivery and assessment of student achievement.
b) Modes of CAI — OFFICIAL SCHEME
★ OFFICIAL SCHEME: "The different modes of CAI are: 1) Drill and practice 2) Tutorial and Dialogues 3) Simulation and games. It should be in brief."
1. Drill and Practice:
  • Presents a series of practice items for already-taught material
  • Immediate feedback on each response — correct or incorrect
  • Builds speed and accuracy through repetition
2. Tutorial and Dialogues:
  • Presents new information, asks questions, and responds to student answers
  • Simulates one-on-one tutoring interaction
  • Branches to remediation or advancement based on performance
3. Simulation and Games:
  • Creates models of real-world systems for safe, risk-free exploration
  • Games add competitive, motivational elements to learning
  • Develops problem-solving, decision-making and strategic thinking
