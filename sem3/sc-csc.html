Q.1
What are the objectives of computer science fair? Explain the steps of organisation of computer science fair.
(5+5 = 10 marks)
▼
a) Objectives of Computer Science Fair (5 marks)
A Computer Science Fair is a co-curricular event where students exhibit their CS projects, innovations, and experiments to demonstrate scientific thinking and creativity.
  • To provide opportunities for independent and self-motivated study in computing
  • To develop scientific temper and problem-solving skills
  • To encourage creativity and innovation among students
  • To recognise talent and motivate bright students
  • To bring the outside world into the classroom environment
  • To develop the spirit of healthy competition
b) Steps of Organisation (5 marks)
  • Central Committee: Overall charge — decides date, venue, time, rules, budgeting
  • Exhibits & Resource Committee: Motivates students for exhibits, sets display rules, arranges rooms
  • Reception Committee: Fixes guests, maintains register for public comments, manages inauguration
  • Judgement Committee: Fixes judges, hospitality, criteria, prepares assessment sheets
  • Arranging Exhibits: Adequate space for each entry; projects of same category placed together; proper labelling
  • Publicity: Wide publicity through media — newspapers, pamphlets, invitation cards
  • Prize Distribution: After evaluation, prizes distributed to winners

Software Problems
  • Virus and malware attacks on systems
  • License expiry of software
  • Compatibility issues with newer OS updates
  • Slow network speeds affecting cloud-based tools
  • Unauthorised installation by students

Q.14
What is an internet? Explain the working principle of internet.
(5 marks)
▼
The Internet is a global network of computers connected via dedicated routers and servers. It enables exchange of text, graphics, voice, video, and programs between any two connected devices worldwide.
Working Principle
  • TCP/IP Protocol: Data is broken into packets; each packet sent independently across the network
  • IP Address: Unique address assigned to each device for identification
  • Routers: Direct data packets along the most efficient path
  • DNS: Translates domain names (google.com) to IP addresses
  • HTTP/HTTPS: Protocol for web browsing and secure data transfer
  • Packets reassembled at destination into original data
Q.15
What is an Algorithm? Write a simple algorithm of solving a problem.
(5 marks)
▼
An Algorithm is a step-by-step, finite, and unambiguous set of instructions to solve a problem or complete a task.
Characteristics
  • Input — takes zero or more inputs
  • Output — produces at least one output
  • Definiteness — each step is clearly defined
  • Finiteness — must terminate after a finite number of steps
  • Effectiveness — each step must be basic and feasible
Example: Algorithm to find the largest of two numbers
Step 1: Start
Step 2: Read two numbers A and B
Step 3: If A > B then print "A is larger"
Step 4: Else print "B is larger"
Step 5: Stop

What are online journal? Explain the stages of online journal publication.
(5 marks)
▼
An Online Journal is a peer-reviewed scholarly publication distributed electronically via the internet. Examples: IEEE Transactions, ACM Digital Library, IJCS.
Stages of Online Journal Publication
  • Manuscript Submission: Author submits article via journal portal
  • Editorial Review: Editor checks relevance and basic quality
  • Peer Review: Two or more experts review the paper anonymously
  • Revision: Author revises based on reviewer comments
  • Acceptance: Editor accepts the revised manuscript
  • Online First: Article published online before print issue
  • Issue Publication: Included in journal volume/issue with DOI assigned
