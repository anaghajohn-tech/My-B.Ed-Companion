Q.4 a) Define Action Research. b) Explain the steps of conducting Action Research in the teaching of mathematics. (2+8) ▾
a) Definition of Action Research (2 marks)
Action Research is a form of investigation designed for use by teachers to attempt to solve problems and improve professional practices in their own classrooms. It involves systematic observations and data collection used in reflection, decision-making and developing effective classroom strategies. — Parsons and Brown, 2002

It is classroom-based research conducted by teachers to solve specific teaching-learning problems in their immediate educational setting.
b) Steps of Action Research in Mathematics Teaching (8 marks)
  • Step 1 — Identification of Problem: The teacher identifies a problem in mathematics teaching through observation, tests, or student feedback. E.g., students unable to solve word problems.
  • Step 2 — Pinpointing the Problem: The general problem is narrowed down to a specific, clearly stated problem. E.g., difficulty in converting word problems to equations.
  • Step 3 — Analyzing Probable Causes: Diagnose why the problem exists — lack of reading comprehension, insufficient practice, poor prior knowledge, poor teaching method, etc.
  • Step 4 — Listing Objectives: Fix clear objectives for what the teacher wants to achieve through the action research.
  • Step 5 — Formulating Hypotheses: State tentative solutions (hypotheses) to address the identified problem. E.g., "Using more worked examples will improve problem-solving."
  • Step 6 — Selection of Tools and Techniques: Choose appropriate tools — tests, observation schedules, worksheets, interviews — to collect data.
  • Step 7 — Collection of Data: Implement the planned action (teaching strategy) and systematically collect data through pre-test and post-test.
  • Step 8 — Analysis and Interpretation: Analyze the collected data using appropriate statistical methods; interpret results.
  • Step 9 — Conclusions and Generalizations: Draw conclusions about the effectiveness of the action taken; check if the hypothesis was supported.
  • Step 10 — Report Writing: Document the process, findings and recommendations so that other teachers can benefit from the experience.

Q.16 Two tangents TP and TQ are drawn to a circle with centre 'O' from the external point 'T'. Prove that ∠PTQ = 2∠OPQ. (5) ▾
